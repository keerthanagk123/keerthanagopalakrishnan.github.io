About

I'm a graduate researcher at Oregon State University working at the intersection of neurotechnology, virtual reality, and machine learning. My thesis investigates how VR can increase embodiment in neuroprosthetic users by estimating cognitive load from physiological signals.

My work spans designing immersive environments in Unreal Engine 5, building ML models for multimodal biosignal analysis (EEG, EDA, PPG), and developing real-time adaptive interfaces. Prior to OSU, I was a research intern at the Indian Institute of Science, where I worked on UAV navigation, path planning, and multi-agent collision avoidance systems.

I'm passionate about building technology that understands and adapts to human cognition — from prosthetics to robotics.
